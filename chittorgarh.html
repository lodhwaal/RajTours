Chittorgarh – The Pride of Mewar

Home Jaipur Jodhpur Jaisalmer Udaipur Pushkar Kota Mount Abu Chittorgarh

Chittorgarh, symbol of Rajput valor and sacrifice, is home to the grand fort of Mewar. The massive hilltop fort complex houses palaces, towers of victory, temples, and stories of legendary figures like Rani Padmini, Maharana Pratap, and Meera Bai.

The fort dates back to the 7th century and became the capital of Mewar. It saw fierce sieges by Alauddin Khilji, Bahadur Shah of Gujarat, and Akbar. Despite battles and sacrifices, it remains a proud symbol of resistance and Rajput spirit.

  • 7th century: Founded by Maurya dynasty rulers.
  • 1303: Alauddin Khilji besieges the fort for Rani Padmini.
  • 16th century: Akbar captures Chittor; Mewar capital shifts to Udaipur.
  • Today: UNESCO World Heritage site, attracting visitors with history, architecture, and legends.

Top Attractions

Chittorgarh Fort – Largest fort in India ➕
Spread over 700 acres, this hill fort features grand gateways, palaces, reservoirs, and temples. It epitomizes Rajput courage and sacrifice.
Vijay Stambh – Tower of Victory ➕
Built in the 15th century by Rana Kumbha to commemorate victory over Malwa, this 9-storey tower is adorned with intricate carvings.
Kirti Stambh – Tower of Fame ➕
A 12th-century Jain tower dedicated to Adinath, the first Tirthankara, showcasing fine Jain sculptures.
Rani Padmini Palace – Palace by the lotus pool ➕
Famous for the tale of Rani Padmini, this palace sits beautifully on water, reflecting Rajput elegance and tragedy.
Meera Temple – Shrine of the poet-saint ➕
Dedicated to Meera Bai, the temple resonates with devotion and features graceful sculptures and carvings.
Best Time: Oct–Mar
Nearest Airport: Udaipur Airport (90 km)
Railway: Chittorgarh Junction
Stay: 1–2 days

Suggested Itinerary

1 Day: Chittorgarh Fort → Vijay Stambh → Kirti Stambh → Rani Padmini Palace → Meera Temple.

2 Days: Day 1 as above, Day 2 explore nearby villages, wildlife sanctuary & light show at the fort.

How to Reach

Air: Udaipur Airport (90 km).
Rail: Chittorgarh Junction connects to major cities.
Road: Well connected by highways to Udaipur, Kota, Bundi, and Jaipur.
