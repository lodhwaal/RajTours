Jaisalmer – The Golden City

Jaisalmer, the Golden City, rises from the Thar desert with honey-colored sandstone walls and narrow lanes. It is famous for its living fort, intricate havelis, desert safaris and rich cultural traditions.

Jaisalmer’s desert region shows traces of prehistoric settlement and trade across the Thar. Founded by Rawal Jaisal in 1156 CE, the city became a vital caravan stop on trade routes between India and Central Asia. Its prosperity led to the construction of magnificent havelis, temples and its iconic fort. The city witnessed Mughal influence, Bhati Rajput rule, British alliances and later integration into independent India.

  • c. 2500–1700 BCE: Harappan contacts in desert fringe, pottery and trade remains.
  • 1156 CE: Rawal Jaisal founds Jaisalmer Fort, desert capital of Bhati Rajputs.
  • 12th–17th c.: Thrives as caravan hub; ornate havelis and Jain temples built.
  • 20th c.: Decline of caravan trade; revival with tourism and desert festivals.
  • 1947–present: Integrated into Rajasthan; famed for camel safaris and cultural heritage.

Top Attractions

Jaisalmer Fort – A living fort with palaces, temples & markets ➕
Built in 1156 CE by Rawal Jaisal, this UNESCO site is among the world’s rare “living forts,” still housing thousands of residents. Inside are palaces, Jain temples, museums and vibrant bazaars.
Patwon Ki Haveli – Ornate cluster of merchant mansions ➕
Collection of five havelis, built in the 19th century by a wealthy trader. Showcasing golden sandstone carvings, balconies, frescoes and mirror work.
Sam Sand Dunes – Desert safari & cultural evenings ➕
Located 40 km from Jaisalmer, Sam Sand Dunes is the heart of desert tourism. Enjoy camel rides, jeep safaris and evening folk performances under starry skies.
Gadisar Lake – Historic water tank with ghats & temples ➕
Built in the 14th century, this lake was once the lifeline of Jaisalmer. Surrounded by temples and ghats, it’s a peaceful spot for boating and photography.
Jain Temples – Intricate temples inside the fort ➕
Beautifully carved temples dedicated to Jain Tirthankaras, noted for sandstone latticework and sculptures dating 12th–15th century.
Best Time: Oct–Feb
Nearest Airport: Jaisalmer Airport (17 km)
Railway: Jaisalmer Station
Stay: 2–3 days

Suggested Itinerary

1 Day: Jaisalmer Fort → Patwon Ki Haveli → Gadisar Lake.

2 Days: Day 1 as above, Day 2 Sam Sand Dunes with desert safari & cultural night.

How to Reach

Air: Jaisalmer Airport (17 km, seasonal flights).
Rail: Jaisalmer Station, connected to Delhi, Jodhpur, Jaipur.
