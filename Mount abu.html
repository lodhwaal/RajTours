About Mount Abu

Mount Abu is Rajasthan’s only hill station, a green refuge in the Aravalli range with cool weather, lakes, temples and panoramic viewpoints. It’s a popular weekend escape from the desert plains.

Historically a retreat for rulers and spiritual seekers, Mount Abu grew around its sacred sites and strategic hilltop positions. The Dilwara temples (11th–13th c.) are masterpieces of marble craftsmanship.

  • Medieval period: Temple building and local settlements.
  • 11th–13th c.: Dilwara Jain temples constructed with exquisite marble carving.
  • British era: Became a favored hill retreat.
  • Today: A mix of pilgrimage, nature, and cool-climate tourism.

Top Attractions

Dilwara Temples – Marble marvels ➕
A group of Jain temples famed for their stunning marble carving, ceilings and pillars — built between the 11th and 13th centuries.
Nakki Lake – Serene boating & promenades ➕
The lake sits in a natural valley, perfect for boat rides, leisurely walks and evenings by the water with cool mountain air.
Guru Shikhar – Highest peak in Aravallis ➕
The summit offers panoramic views and a shrine; it's a short drive and walk up to the peak — ideal at sunrise.
Sunset Point – Spectacular hilltop sunsets ➕
Short walk to a viewpoint that overlooks valleys and rocky outcrops — popular for evening gatherings and photography.
Achalgarh Fort – Historic fort & temples ➕
Once a strategic fort, Achalgarh's ruins and nearby temples make for a scenic half-day trip with great views.
Best Time: Oct–Feb
Nearest Airport: Udaipur Airport (160 km)
Railway: Abu Road Junction (28 km)
Stay: 1–3 days

Suggested Itinerary

1 Day: Dilwara Temples → Nakki Lake → Sunset Point.

2 Days: Day 1 as above, Day 2 Guru Shikhar → Achalgarh Fort → local markets.

How to Reach

Air: Udaipur Airport (160 km).
Rail: Abu Road Junction (28 km).
Road: Accessible by well-maintained hill roads from Udaipur and Ahmedabad.
