Kumbhalgarh – The Great Wall of India

Home Jaipur Jodhpur Jaisalmer Udaipur Kumbhalgarh Pushkar Kota Chittorgarh

Kumbhalgarh Fort, perched in the Aravalli hills, is renowned for its massive 36-km long wall – the second longest in the world after the Great Wall of China. Built by Rana Kumbha in the 15th century, it was the birthplace of Maharana Pratap.

The fort, built around 1443 CE, was a strategic stronghold of Mewar. Its walls enclose over 300 temples and palaces, and it remained nearly unconquered. Declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2013, it stands as a testament to Rajput military architecture.

  • 6th–12th c.: Early settlements and small fortifications in Aravallis.
  • 1443 CE: Rana Kumbha constructs Kumbhalgarh Fort.
  • 16th c.: Birthplace of Maharana Pratap.
  • 16th–19th c.: Served as refuge for Mewar rulers during wars.
  • 2013: Inscribed as UNESCO World Heritage Site under Hill Forts of Rajasthan.

Top Attractions

The Great Wall – 36 km of defense ➕
The wall stretches 36 km with massive stone battlements, second only to the Great Wall of China.
Jatin Temple – Ancient temple inside fort ➕
One of the many temples in the fort complex, reflecting intricate Rajput architecture and devotion.
Vedi Temple – Built by Rana Kumbha ➕
A rare Jain temple built by Rana Kumbha, unique for its octagonal design and historical significance.
