About Jaipur

Jaipur, the capital of Rajasthan, is famous for its pink-hued old city, royal palaces, forts, and lively markets. A part of the Golden Triangle, it combines heritage with modern charm.

Under colonial rule Jaipur remained a princely state; after independence it became Rajasthan‚Äôs capital and a modern heritage city.

Top Attractions

Amber Fort ‚Äì Hilltop fort with elephant rides & evening light show ‚ûï
Built in 1592 by Raja Man Singh I, Amber Fort is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most important Rajput forts in India. It blends Hindu and Mughal styles with massive gates, courtyards, and palaces. The Sheesh Mahal (Mirror Palace) dazzles with thousands of reflective pieces. The Diwan-i-Aam and Sukh Niwas demonstrate ingenious design, with water channels used for natural cooling. Elephant rides to the fort and the evening sound-and-light show make it one of Jaipur‚Äôs most memorable experiences.
Hawa Mahal ‚Äì Iconic palace with 953 latticed windows ‚ûï
Constructed in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh, Hawa Mahal (Palace of Winds) is Jaipur‚Äôs most iconic landmark. Its five-story pink sandstone fa√ßade resembles a honeycomb and has 953 jharokhas (latticed windows), designed to allow royal women to watch festivals and city life unseen. The structure represents the crown of Lord Krishna and exemplifies Rajput artistry. Inside, narrow passages and small chambers ensure ventilation, keeping the palace cool even in summer. A museum inside exhibits miniature paintings, armory, and artifacts.
Jantar Mantar ‚Äì UNESCO astronomical observatory ‚ûï
Built in 1734 by Maharaja Jai Singh II, Jantar Mantar is an astronomical observatory and UNESCO World Heritage Site. It houses 19 large-scale instruments for tracking celestial bodies, predicting eclipses, and measuring time. The highlight is the Samrat Yantra, the world‚Äôs largest stone sundial, accurate to within 2 seconds. Jai Singh II‚Äôs vision of combining science and culture is preserved here, making it one of the most important observatories in India.
Nahargarh Fort ‚Äì Famous for panoramic city views & sunsets ‚ûï
Built in 1734 by Sawai Jai Singh II, Nahargarh Fort crowns the Aravalli hills overlooking Jaipur. It served as both a retreat and a defensive bastion. Madhavendra Bhawan inside contains a series of suites for the king and his queens, each connected by corridors. According to legend, the fort is named after Nahar Singh Bhomia, a spirit believed to haunt the site. Nahargarh offers the most stunning sunset views over Jaipur and played a role in sheltering locals during the 1857 uprising.
Bazaars ‚Äì Johari, Bapu & Tripolia markets for gems & textiles ‚ûï
Jaipur‚Äôs bazaars are a window into its living culture. Johari Bazaar is world-famous for its gemstones, meenakari work, and gold jewelry. Bapu Bazaar showcases colorful textiles, bandhani fabrics, handicrafts, perfumes, and traditional mojari shoes. Tripolia Bazaar is known for lacquer bangles, brassware, and ironware. These vibrant markets preserve centuries-old craftsmanship including block printing, blue pottery, and stone carving, making them essential stops for visitors seeking authentic Rajasthani heritage.
