Jaipur’s history stretches from the **Matsya Mahajanapada** and tribal Meena rule to Kachwaha ascendancy, and later to the founding of the planned Pink City.

  • Matsya Mahajanapada (c. 6th–4th c. BCE): Jaipur–Amber was part of the Matsya polity with early settlements and trade in Aravalli foothills.
  • Meena rule (pre-11th c.): Meena chieftains controlled Amber and surrounding hills before Kachwaha arrival; forts and tribal governance thrived.
  • Kachwaha / Amber (11th–17th c.): Kachwaha Rajputs made Amber their capital; culture and architecture flourished.
  • 1727: Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II founded Jaipur, India’s first planned city.
  • 1876: City painted pink to honor Prince of Wales’ visit, earning the name “Pink City.”
  • 1949: Jaipur merged into Rajasthan after India’s independence.

Top Attractions

Amber Fort – Hilltop fort ➕
Built in 1592 by Raja Man Singh, Amber Fort blends Hindu and Mughal architecture. The Sheesh Mahal and Diwan-i-Aam are highlights.
Hawa Mahal – Palace of Winds ➕
Constructed in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh, Hawa Mahal has 953 latticed windows for royal ladies to view the streets unseen.
Jantar Mantar – Astronomical observatory ➕
Built in 1734 by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, UNESCO-listed with the world’s largest stone sundial.
Nahargarh Fort – Hill fort ➕
Built in 1734, provided defense and now famous for panoramic sunset views.
Bazaars – Johari & Bapu Bazaar ➕
Jaipur’s bazaars offer gemstones, textiles, handicrafts, jewelry. Johari for gems, Bapu for mojris & souvenirs.
Best Time: Oct–Mar
Nearest Airport: Jaipur International Airport (12 km)
Railway: Jaipur Junction
Stay: 2–3 days

Suggested Itinerary

1 Day: Amber Fort → Hawa Mahal → Jantar Mantar → City Palace → Bazaars.

2 Days: Day 1 as above, Day 2 visit Nahargarh Fort, Jal Mahal, Albert Hall Museum.
