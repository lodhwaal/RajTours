Jaipur – The Pink City

Jaipur’s region has been inhabited since ancient times. Archaeological traces link it to the late Harappan/Indus cultural zone; indigenous tribal communities — notably the Meena — shaped early settlement, agriculture and governance. Over centuries the area saw Vedic, Mauryan and Gupta influence, Rajput consolidation at Amber, Mughal alliances, and the planned founding of Jaipur by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II in 1727. Under colonial rule Jaipur remained a princely state; after independence it became Rajasthan’s capital and a modern heritage city.

  • c. 2500–1700 BCE: Indus/Harappan cultural traces near Aravallis/Bairat.
  • c. 1500–300 BCE: Matsya Mahajanapada; early Vedic activity; Meena tribal presence.
  • 321 BCE–550 CE: Mauryan & Gupta periods — Buddhist sites, Ashokan edicts.
  • 7th–16th c.: Rajput rise; Kachwaha rule at Amber; Meena agrarian role continues.
  • 16th–18th c.: Mughal–Kachwaha alliance; Amber Fort expanded.
  • 1727 CE: Sawai Jai Singh II founds Jaipur — India’s first major planned city.
  • 19th–20th c.: Princely state under British; painted pink in 1876.
  • 1949–present: Rajasthan capital; UNESCO World Heritage City (2019).

Top Attractions

Amber Fort – Hilltop fort with elephant rides & light show ➕
Built in 1592 by Raja Man Singh I, Amber Fort is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most important Rajput forts. Sheesh Mahal dazzles with thousands of mirrors, Diwan-i-Aam and Sukh Niwas show ingenious design with natural cooling. Elephant rides and the evening sound-and-light show make it unforgettable.
Hawa Mahal – Palace with 953 latticed windows ➕
Built in 1799, Hawa Mahal’s five-story façade resembles a honeycomb. Its 953 jharokhas let royal women watch city life unseen. The design ensures ventilation, keeping it cool in summer. A small museum inside displays miniature paintings and artifacts.
Jantar Mantar – UNESCO observatory ➕
Built in 1734, Jantar Mantar has 19 giant instruments for tracking stars, predicting eclipses and measuring time. The Samrat Yantra, the world’s largest stone sundial, is accurate to within 2 seconds.
Nahargarh Fort – Sunset & city views ➕
Built in 1734, Nahargarh overlooks Jaipur from the Aravalli hills. Madhavendra Bhawan contains suites for the king and queens. The fort is linked with legend of Nahar Singh Bhomia. It gives the best sunset views of the city.
Bazaars – Johari, Bapu & Tripolia markets ➕
Johari Bazaar is famous for gemstones and meenakari. Bapu Bazaar has colorful textiles, perfumes and mojari shoes. Tripolia Bazaar is known for lacquer bangles and brassware. Craft traditions like block printing and blue pottery thrive here.
Best Time: Oct–Mar
Nearest Airport: Jaipur Intl. (12 km)
Railway: Jaipur Junction
Stay: 2–3 days

Suggested Itinerary

1 Day: Amber Fort → City Palace & Jantar Mantar → Hawa Mahal & bazaars.

2 Days: Day 1 as above, Day 2 explore Nahargarh, Albert Hall Museum & shopping.
