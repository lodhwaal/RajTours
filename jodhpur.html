About Jodhpur

Jodhpur, gateway to the Thar, is called the Blue City for the vivid indigo-painted houses around Mehrangarh Fort. It is the historic capital of Marwar and blends royal palaces, sandstone forts and bustling bazaars.

The Jodhpur region has long-settled landscapes with archaeological traces linking it to ancient north-western Indian cultural zones. Local clans, trade routes across the Thar and control of water sources shaped early settlement. The Rathore dynasty established Jodhpur in 1459 under Rao Jodha; the city later expanded with major contributions from rulers like Jaswant Singh and Maharaja Umaid Singh. Under British-era princely administration, Jodhpur retained cultural autonomy and developed modern institutions. After independence it became part of Rajasthan and today is an important cultural, tourist and commercial hub.
